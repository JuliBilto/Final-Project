Internship Outfits

Now that you have landed the internship, it is important to know what to wear. You always want to make a lasting impression so everday is a fashion show. Below are some outfit ideas. Each outfit has accesories that take the boring out of a traditional buisness casual look.

 

What Not to Wear to an Internship

 

Super Loud Pieces


Start with a basic black dress or a pants-and-blazer combo— whatBerger calls a “blank canvas” outfit. With simple, minimalist pieces, you’re free to have a little fun with your accessories. Show off your personality with statement necklaces or a colorful shoe. We love Berger’s perfect black dress here!


Flashy Clothing


Stay away from loud or flashy clothing at your internship. “You want them to remember you because you were reliable, loyal, professional, and hardworking—not because you had on a bedazzled blazer,” Berger says. You can absolutely show your personal style, but remember your internship isn’t a fashion show.


Party Attire


Anything that you would even think of wearing to a party or club is out. Just don’t go there! Huge no-no’s according to Berger? Tiny tops, cut-out dresses, crop tops, smokey eyes, and super sexy pumps. Save them for the weekend!
Tight Clothes
“If you look like you’re dressing up for Halloween as a ‘naughty secretary’ or ‘schoolgirl,’ you're wearing the wrong attire,” Bergerwarns. Button-down shirts shouldn’t be too tight or show cleavage, skirts shouldn’t be too tight or short, and makeup should be simple. Save the sexy look for October 31st!


Unprofessional Shoes


While you may be able to rock flats everyday once you actually secure the internship, Berger says to go with heels for your interview—a basic black pump is always in style. Heels look professional, sophisticated, and put-together. But, save your soles! Especially if you live in a city, bring flats to walk to and from your interview in.


Crazy Hairstyles


For interviews, keep your hair simple. Try styles like a topknot or a high pony! Skip wacky styles—you want people to notice you, not your unusual ‘do. “I like simple hair for internships. Either put it back, or wear it down and straight. If you want to add some curl, that’s fine—just don’t go overboard. This isn’t the runway!” Berger says.


Heavy Makeup


“You want to look fresh and flawless,” Berger says. “Stick to basic makeup.” Your internship is not the place to test-drive those crazy runway makeup trends.Think foundation, powder, mascara, and light blush. A bright lip is OK if your office isn’t too conservative, but be sure to keep the rest of your outfit and makeup extra simple.


Outfits That Don't Fit The Office Culture


Once you spend a few days working, you’ll get a feel for the unofficial "dress code" in the office. Take cues from the other women—if they always wear heels, you should too. Or if they never wear sleeveless tops without a cardigan, then you shouldn’t either. It is possible to look cute and professional at your internship, like Seventeen editor Devin. The cut of her dress is uber-professional (knee-length and no cleavage) but still brings the fun with it's bright color!

Intern Must-Haves

 

Every intern needs:

  • Watch - A watch is great for keeping track of time and managing multple projects.
  • Durable Tote Bag - Some internships require you to bring a laptop. A durable bag will make commutes a little less miserable.
  • Cute Phone Case - No reason really but why not?
  • Smartphone- When you are on the goyou can ask Siri for help. Maybe you need directions to the nearest Starbucks.
